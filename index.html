WARNING: SIMULATION DISCLAIMER

This is a computational model and educational tool only. All scenarios, projections, outcomes, and strategic assessments are simulated estimates — not predictions, intelligence assessments, or financial advice.

WHAT THIS IS

An interactive barrel-by-barrel economic simulation of the 2026 US-Iran conflict and its global energy market impact. Variables are seeded from verified public sources (EIA, IEA, Bruegel, Bloomberg, Reuters, Al Jazeera, CNBC) but the model outputs are inherently uncertain projections based on simplified economic relationships.

WHAT THIS IS NOT

Not intelligence. This contains no classified, restricted, or proprietary data. All constants are derived from publicly available journalism and research.
Not financial advice. Nothing here constitutes investment, trading, or financial advice. Energy market forecasts carry high uncertainty, especially during active conflicts.
Not journalism. The news feed surfaces third-party articles. We do not verify, endorse, or take editorial responsibility for linked content.

DATA SOURCES

• EIA STEO Mar 2026 — US production • Bruegel Mar 2026 — China supply • Foreign Policy Mar 17 — Hormuz flows • CNBC Mar 19 — LNG crisis • Kpler/DiscoveryAlert — China reserves • ING Think Mar 2026 — Asia GDP

GEOPOLITICAL SENSITIVITY

This models an active, ongoing armed conflict. Casualty figures, territorial control, and political developments are not represented here. This tool focuses exclusively on energy economics — not military strategy or human cost.

News feed: Uses GDELT Doc API with a tone filter (toneabs < 3) to surface near-neutral international coverage. Articles reflect their own publications editorial positions, not ours.

LIMITATIONS

GDP elasticity models are simplified linear approximations. Real-world responses involve lags, policy reactions, and non-linear dynamics not captured here. China SPR figures carry high uncertainty. All probabilities shown are illustrative estimates, not actuarial calculations.
